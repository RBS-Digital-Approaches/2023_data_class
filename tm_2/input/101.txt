January 19 , 1946 OBITUARY Dr. C. E. M'Clung Zoologist , 75 , Dies Special to THE NEW YORK TIMES PHILADELPHIA , Jan. 18 -- Dr. Clarence Erwin McClung , Emeritus Professor of Zoology at the University of Pennsylvania and internationally know for his extensive study of chromosome , die of a heart attack yesterday in his home at near-by Swarthmore. His age be 75. Born in Clayton , Calif. , Dr. McClung be graduate from the University of Kansas in 1892. He begin his teach career there in 1893 , remain until 1912 , when he come to the University of Pennsylvania a Professor of Zoology and director of the Zoological Laboratory. Following his retirement from teach in 1940 , Dr. McClung spend one year at the University of Illinois a act head of it Department of Zoology. At his death he be act head of the Department of Biology at Swarthmore College , substitute for Dr. Laurence Irving , who have enter military service. Honored by Former Students Upon his completion of twenty-five year of teaching , approximately 100 of his former student join in a testimonial to his `` profound influence on individual and organization concern with biological research. '' In 1940 the University of Pennsylvania confer upon him at it bicentennial year commencement exercise the honorary degree of Doctor of Science. As reflect in his publication , Dr. McClung 's interest and activity cover a wide range of subject matter , but it be for his writing on the chromosome and their relation to heredity and the classification and evolution of animal that he be know around the world. He be the first to recognize the relation of chromosome to the determination of sex , have discover in 1902 , through his study of the germ cell of grasshopper , that a difference exist in the chromosomal constitution of the two sexes. In 1930 he represent the United States Government and various scientific organization at the International Biological Congress at Montevideo , Uruguay. In 1933-34 the Rockefeller Foundation send him a visiting professor at the Institute of Preventative Medicine , Keio University , Tokyo. Dr. McClung leave a widow , Mrs. Anna Drake McClung ; two daughter , Mrs. Beth Bullis of Detroit and Mrs. Ruth Jones , a member of the faculty of Swarthmore College ; three grandchild and a sister , Mrs. W. R. Childs of Eldorado , Kan. Explained Mendel 's Law Basis Dr. McClung be know throughout the world for his extensive study on chromosome , the `` pod '' that carry the gene , unit of heredity. He succeed in explain the mechanism underlie Mendel 's Law , just a Mendel himself give the first acceptable explanation of inheritance a set forth in the Darwinian theory of evolution. He receive from the University of Kansas a Ph. G. degree in 1892 , and A. B. in 1896 , an A. M. in 1898 and a Ph. D. in 1902. He take graduate work at Columbia and Chicago Universities and also hold an honorary degree from Franklin and Marshall College. He be Assistant Professor of Zoology at the University of Kansas from 1897 , to 1900 , associate professor from 1900 to 1906 , professor from 1906 to 1912 , head of the department and curator of vertebrate and paleontology collection from 1902 to 1912 , and act dean of the School of Medicine from 1902 to 1906. Headed Scientific Expeditions He be a member of the embryology staff and since 1914 a trustee of Woods Hole , Mass. He also have head scientific expedition to Oregon , Washington , western Kansas , China , Java , Ceylon , South America and South Africa. Dr. McClung serve a chairman of the Division of Biology and Agriculture of the National Research Council , Washington , from 1919 to 1921 , and be a member of the advisory board of Wistar Institute and Morris Arboretum. He be vice president of Section F of the American Association for the Advancement of Science in 1926 , and president of the American Zoology Society in 1910 , American Society of Naturalists in 1927 , Union of American Biological Societies from 1922 to 1930 , and Sigma Xi from 1919 to 1921. He be the author of `` Microscopical Technique , '' `` Chromosome Theory of Heredity '' and technical paper on cytology , sex-determination and paleontology. He be manage editor of The Journal of Morphology , a member of the board of editor of Acta Zoologica , Cytologia and president of the board of Biological Abstracts from 1925 to 1933. His club include the Cosmos , Lenape and University of Illinois .
