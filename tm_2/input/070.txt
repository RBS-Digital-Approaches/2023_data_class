October 4 , 1936 OBITUARY John W. Heisman , Noted Coach , Dies By THE NEW YORK TIMES John William Heisman , all-around authority on athletics and former football coach , die yesterday in his home at 28 East Seventieth Street after a brief illness. He be 66 year old. For the last six year he have be physical director of the Downtown Athletic Club. Mr. Heisman for more than thirty-five year be football coach at a number of lead colleges. In 1923 and 1924 he be president of the American Football Coaches Association. He be the originator of the `` Heisman shift '' and be say to be the first to suggest the introduction of the forward pas into football. He be also the inventor of many method use in modern football. From 1904 to 1920 he coach football , baseball and basketball at the Georgia Institute of Technology , where he develop the famous `` Golden Tornado '' teams. He begin his coach career in 1892 at Oberlin College. In 1893 he coach all sport at the University of Akron. From 1895 to 1900 he coach football and baseball at Alabama Polytechnic Institute , and from 1900 to 1904 be coach at Clemson College. In 1908 he be director of athletics at the Atlanta Athletic Club. From 1910 to 1914 he be president of the Atlanta Baseball Association. In 1920 he coach football at the University of Pennsylvania and in 1923 fill the same position at Washington and Jefferson College. From 1924 to 1927 he be head football coach and director of athletics at Rice Institute , Houston , Texas. In 1888 he be a member of the Brown football team , and in 1889 of the Pennsylvania varsity football eleven. He be bear in Cleveland , the son of John M. and Sarah Lehr Heisman. He be educate at Brown University and the University of Pennsylvania , where he receive his LL. B. degree in 1892. Mr. Heisman be the author of `` Principles of Football. '' As physical director of the Downtown Athletic Club , he lecture there last year at weekly football forums. He predict then that there would be less fumbling of lateral pass this year. His widow , Mrs. Edith Maora Cole Heisman , survives. Burial will be in Rhinelander , Wis .
