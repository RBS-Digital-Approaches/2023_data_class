November 5 , 1955 OBITUARY Cy Young Is Dead ; Famed Pitcher , 88 BY THE NEW YORK TIMES NEWCOMERSTOWN , Ohio , Nov. 4 ( AP ) -- Denton True ( Cy ) Young , one of baseball 's great pitcher , die today at the age of 88. A member of baseball 's Hall of Fame , Mr. Young be stricken while sit in a chair in the home of Mr. and Mrs. John Benedum , with whom he make his home near here. His Feats Unequaled Traded to the major from the minor for a suit of clothing , Cy Young thrill the baseball world from 1890 to 1911 with a blaze fast ball that set pitching record still unequaled. Six foot two inch tall and weigh 210 pound , this Ohio farmer pitch and win more game than any major leaguer. When he retire at the age of 45 because his leg have weaken , he have win 511 of 826 decision in both league and for five teams. In all , he hurl in 906 games. As the start pitcher for the Boston Red Sox in 1903 , Mr. Young throw the first pitch in a world series game. Fifty year later , a a guest at the opening game of the world series between the Brooklyn Dodgers and New York Yankees , he stand in the pitcher 's box and throw a ceremonial strike to the Yankee catcher , Yogi Berra , to open the series. He be 86 at the time. During his career , he be a thirty-game winner five season ; a twenty-game victor sixteen times. He pitch one perfect game , two other no-hit shutout and perform the `` iron man '' feat of hurling and win complete game of a doubleheader. One of the early member of the baseball Hall of Fame at Cooperstown , N.Y. , Mr. Young pitch for the Cleveland Nationals , St. Louis Cardinals , Red Sox and Cleveland Indians and Boston Braves. He win 291 National League game and 220 in the American. Had 36-10 Mark in 1892 For fourteen consecutive year , begin in 1891 , Mr. Young win twenty or more games. The 1892 season , when he post a 36-10 record , be his best. Mr. Young 's feat of pitch twenty-three consecutive hitless inning over a four-game span early in 1904 still stand a a major league record. His total of 2,836 strikeout be surpass only by the late Walter Johnson 's 3,497. He be bear on a farm in Gilmore , Ohio , on March 29 , 1867. While pitch for the Canton ( Ohio ) club of the old Tri-State League in 1890 , Mr. Young be nickname Cy. `` I think I have to show all my stuff , '' he recall year later , `` and I almost tear the board off the grandstand with my fast ball. One of the fellow call me 'Cyclone , ' but finally shorten it to 'Cy , ' and it 's be that ever since. '' The league disband during the 1890 season and the pitcher join the Cleveland Nationals early in August of that year. Pitched , Won Doubleheader Still a gawky country boy , he make his major league debut against Cap Anson 's Chicago White Stockings and win the game. Mr. Young win 10 and lose 7 for Cleveland during the late stage of the season. Two of those victory be obtain on Oct. 4 , when he capture both end of a doubleheader against Philadelphia. In his first complete major league campaign the next year , Mr. Young win 27 and lose 22 games. For the next thirteen season he stay above the .500 mark. He pitch his first major league no- hit , no-run contest on Sept. 18 , 1897 , blank Cincinnati , 6 to 0. His affiliation with Cleveland end after the 1898 campaign and he play with the St. Louis Cardinals in 1899 and 1900. Mr. Young begin eight year with the Red Sox in 1901. While blank Philadelphia on May 5 , 1904 , he do not permit an oppose runner to reach first base. It be the third perfect game in major league history. On June 30 , 1908 , he win his third no-hit , no-run decision in the major by shut out New York , 8 to 0. He be trade to the Cleveland Indians in 1909 , and in the middle of the 1911 campaign be trade to the Braves. He retire to his farm near Peoli , Ohio , after that season .
